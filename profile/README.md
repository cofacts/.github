# Cofacts: A Grassroots Fact-Checking Movement

## What is Cofacts?

**Cofacts** (真的假的 / "Really?") is Taiwan's pioneering **crowd-sourced fact-checking platform** that combines chatbot technology with civic collaboration to combat misinformation. Founded in **2016** by engineers and volunteers from the g0v civic tech community, it represents one of the world's earliest fact-checking chatbot initiatives.

## Mission & Approach

Cofacts operates on a **hybrid human-AI model**:

- **24/7 Chatbot Response**: Users can forward suspicious messages, images, or URLs to the LINE chatbot for instant preliminary responses.
- **Crowd-Sourced Verification**: A community of volunteers researches and fact-checks submitted content.
- **Open Database**: All fact-check reports are publicly accessible, creating a transparent knowledge base.

## Organizational Structure

### Origins
Cofacts began as a **g0v (零時政府)** civic tech project — Taiwan's renowned open-source community where citizens build tools for public good.

### Evolution
- **2016–2024**: Volunteer-driven community project supported by the **Open Culture Foundation (OCF)** 
- **2024 onwards**: Transitioned into a **formal non-profit association**, with co-founder **Billion Lee (比鄰)** leading organizational development to ensure sustainable operations

## Core Values

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Open Source** | All code publicly available on GitHub; transparent development process |
| **Civic Collaboration** | Anyone can contribute fact-checks, regardless of professional background |
| **Democratic Resilience** | Empowering citizens to verify information and strengthen public trust |
| **Media Literacy** | Regular workshops and educational initiatives to build critical thinking skills |

## Impact

Cofacts has become a critical infrastructure in Taiwan's information ecosystem, particularly during election periods and public health crises. By enabling **grassroots participation in fact-checking**, it demonstrates how civic technology can foster democratic resilience against disinformation .
